Time Play

Time Play
Northside Festival, 2017

Course: Data Aesthetics and Information Visualization Instructor: Christian Swinehart This web app explores time and its behaviors as physical symbols. Data is sourced from Brooklyn's 2017 Northside Festival. Data analysis was performed in RStudio. The visualized result returns a simple rectangle shape which, upon hover, returns the number of hours played at each venue in number of 360 degree counter-clockwise spins.

Launch in Desktop Only

If you're not wrinkling time or passing time or losing time, you're playing with time.

This exercise focused on using fonts as the dominant expression of information hierarchy. Instructions were to limit ourselves to one font-family: Libre-Franklin to organize the information scraped from Brooklyn's Northside Festival page. Information included shows, artists, venues, dates, times and duration. The design hoped to act as a "visual summary calculator" where you can gain a ballpark of how many minutes were played at each venue. Each venue opens a container listing the artists who played at the venue. In the final deliverable, a square rotates to express number of hours played. Each rotation (1 sec to 1 hour) provides a visual ballpark estimate of length. I broke the rules slightly by expressing duration as a shape instead of soley font size, although I could argue the rectangles are simply visually manipulated versions of the letter I.
