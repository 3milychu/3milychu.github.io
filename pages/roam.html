ROAM

00

Project Objective

ROAM is an gesture-based artificial intelligence ring that helps people get in touch with their emotions and intuitions. After setting a goal, the wearer uses ROAM's warm and cool indicator to intuit how close they are to achieving their goal.

01

Target Demographics

Technology today is making people exhausted. People are constantly trying to disconnect and get away from their screens. In a study conducted by the American Psychological Association (APA) in 2017, 19% don’t allow devices during time with friends, 21% don’t allow devices during family time, 21% “Unplug” or take a “digital detox” periodically, and 28% don’t allow cell phones at the dinner table. ROAM's primary target market are technology users who are aware of and seeking to actively combat the effects of technology on their mental health, particularly on their ability to authentically connect with themselves and others in the real world. This is led by millenials and Gen X, followed by Boomers and Matures.

02

Why ROAM?

ROAM works to strengthen authentic human and environmental connection through technology by augmenting physicial indicators of intuition. This was inspired by studies revealing the power of physiological intuition. In one example, researchers asked their subjects to play a card game where the goal was to win the most money. What the subjects did not realize, however, is that the game was rigged from the start. There were two stacks of cards to choose from; one you’ll lose, one you win. It took about 50 cards before the subjects said they had a hunch about which deck was safer. It took about 80 cards before they could actually explain the difference between the two decks. However, after only 10 cards, the sweat glands on the subjects’ palms unconsciously opened slightly every time they reached for a card in the dangerous deck. Long before the analytical brain can explain a discovered preference, our bodily intuition can sense preferences and higher probability of reaching favorable outcomes.

03

Specs

ROAM uses biometric, motion, sound and visual data inputs to create a database from which it can learn about an individual's behaviors and surrounding environments. When a goal is set, the desired composition of all data inputs is optimized according to the learning model built by ROAM. The warm and cool indicators intuit to the wearer how close they are to that optimal level, or how close they are to achieving the goal they've set.
