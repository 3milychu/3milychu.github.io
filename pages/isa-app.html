Project Objective

The Jain Family Institute is a think tank that designs and implements social initiatives such as Universal Basic Income and Income Share Agreements. For this web app, the goal was to use their data models to show the difference in lifetime income, payments and consumption using different borrowing instruments.

01

Data Preparation

The data visualized used the institute's native .csv file, which lists series, the amounts by age 22 to 100 attached to that series as well as other information related to the series such as income level, level of risk aversion and amount borrowed. To maintain the dataset in this format, I cleaned it in python and extracted the amounts by age 22 to 66 for each series I would have to call in javascript.

02

Design Iterations

Every week for about 6 weeks, I met with JFI's editorial content and ISA research team leads to iterate on the design towards the targeted functionality and data story. We began with barebones design to inform a test build, working up to more sophisticated designs for live prototypes hosted on github pages.
