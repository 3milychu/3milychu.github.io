Quantitative

Met.erials
Parsons MSDV Major Studio, 2018

Instructor: Richard The
This project analyzed 155k objects containing the Metropolitan Musem of Art's top seven occuring materials. Questions asked: What are representative items made from each material in the MET's collection? What larger patterns are they a part of, and what can we learn from the semiology of cultural artifacts?

Tools Background This project was created in the Major Studio I course within Parson's MS Data Visualization program in partnership with the Metropolitan Museum of Art in New York City, NY. We worked with the museum's General Manager of Collection Information to understand the nature of and nuances behind the data - including missing gaps and where the most reliable information was recorded consistently in the dataset. Qualitative

Epoch Runway
Parsons MSDV Major Studio, 2018

Instructor: Richard The
In this project, the experience is driven by information on all costume objects in the Metropolitan Museum of Art. The UI is designed around revealing patterns around the 7.6k items available, with relevant metadata revealed on hover in all instances and linked to originals on the MET's website.

Tools Process Data Analysis: Reclass in R by Body Part Image Extraction: Batch Image Extraction in Python Mockups of Scenes Github Repo Overview Landing Page Provides a high-level view of all the types of objects available: Headwear, Tops, Bottoms, Ensembles, Footwear and Accessories. Ensemble Catwalk A data vis take on the traditional catwalk, where an algorithm randomly selects an outfit to display in a computational art scene. Brunches of Suits Creates a playful setting to explore a subset of costume objects - suits. Metadata on culture is processed and translated into food faces. Experimental Ornamentals A motion fashion collage using limbs to display the different types of accessories that humans have adorned over the ages.
